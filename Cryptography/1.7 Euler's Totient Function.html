1.7 Euler's Totient Function

Euler's totient function, usually written as φ(n) (phi of n) counts The number of positive integers less than or equal to n that are coprime to n

Finding φ(n) for a prime

If p is a prime number, then all positive integers smaller than p are coprime with it. Therefore: φ(p) = p − 1
